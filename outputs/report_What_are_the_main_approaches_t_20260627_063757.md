# Literature Review on Building AI Agents Capable of Reasoning and Acting

## Executive Summary
The development of AI agents that can effectively reason and act encompasses several key approaches, notably grounded in theories of agency, diverse architectural frameworks, and the integration of tool and function use. Key methodologies include designing cooperative architectures for efficient multi-agent communication and employing empirical evaluations to refine these systems. Ongoing challenges relate to dynamic environment requirements and the integration of theoretical frameworks with practical implementations.

## Introduction
As artificial intelligence systems become increasingly integrated into various domains, understanding how to build AI agents that can reason and act is critical. The ability for AI agents to not only simulate understanding but effectively operate in complex, dynamic environments hinges on foundational theories, architectural designs, and the methodological evaluations that guide their development. This literature review synthesizes key insights from a corpus of research papers to clarify existing architectures and strategies while identifying significant challenges in advancing AI capabilities in reasoning and action.

## Methodology
This literature review is based on a curated corpus of 15 papers, selected through a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) process. The review synthesizes themes across the selected literature, examining critical facets of reasoning and action in AI agents and organizing findings to highlight commonalities and divergent perspectives.

## Findings

### Theoretical Foundations
The theoretical underpinnings of AI agents focus on concepts such as agency, dynamic logic, and speech act theory. Wooldridge (1995) emphasizes that having an intention to act increases the likelihood of agent actions, anchoring much of the discourse on how agency can be framed within logical systems. These foundational theories serve as benchmarks for understanding agent behavior and design within AI architectures.

### Architectural Approaches
Architectures for AI agents range widely, from frameworks emphasizing reactive systems to those featuring more complex, layered designs like the Intelligent Resource-bounded Machine Architecture (IRMA) discussed by Wooldridge (1995). Recent architectures such as ReAct and AutoGen build upon these foundations, incorporating tool use and collaborative dialogue management to enhance decision-making capabilities. The role of component modularity in architecture is highlighted as crucial for effective reasoning in complex situations.

### Tool Use and Function Calling
Significant enhancements in the reasoning capabilities of AI agents have been attributed to the integration of tool use and function calling. Research indicates that these mechanisms facilitate access to external knowledge, thereby enriching the contextual basis upon which agents make decisions (ReAct et al., 2023; AutoGen et al., 2023).

### Multi-Agent Coordination
The literature identifies multi-agent coordination as essential for successful interaction in complex environments. Strategies for distributed reasoning, as explored in CAMEL (2023) and Xi (2023), reinforce how effective communication and negotiation among agents improve collaboration and resolve conflicts. Cooperative agent architectures are noted as pivotal in establishing efficient interaction protocols.

### Challenges and Future Directions
Challenges persist in ensuring the adaptability of agents within dynamic environments and balancing between theory and practical applications. Wooldridge (1995) highlights limitations in agent designs, including safety and performance consistency. The integration of theoretical models into operational frameworks remains under-explored, necessitating further research on standardized evaluation methods for comparing agent efficacy in real-world scenarios.

## Discussion
The findings illustrate a consensus regarding the importance of foundational theories, architectural diversity, and cooperative mechanisms in agent design. However, debates arise about the translation of theoretical principles into actionable implementations, particularly in the effectiveness of reinforcement learning across various multi-agent applications. The observed gap in addressing cultural and ethical implications within agent communications indicates a crucial area for future inquiry.

## Conclusion
This review has demonstrated that building AI agents capable of reasoning and acting relies on a multi-faceted approach that draws on established theories, innovative architectural designs, and effective coordination strategies. Future research must tackle existing challenges, particularly focused on enhancing adaptability and fostering a more explicit linkage between theory and practice, while also considering the broader societal implications of AI agent behavior.
